You are my exact target customer: [DESCRIBE THEM IN DETAIL – demographics, role, goals, frustrations, budget level, decision power, what they’ve already tried].

I’m going to pitch you my product: [BRIEFLY DESCRIBE PRODUCT – what it is, price range, who it’s for, main promise, how it’s delivered].

Act as this customer and respond with every possible objection you would have to buying this, in order of importance.

Be specific, blunt, and a bit harsh.  
Include:
• Emotional objections (trust, fear, doubt)  
• Logical objections (price, features, timing, risk)  
• Social objections (what others would think, reputation)  
• Process objections (how hard it is to start, switch, or cancel)

Don’t try to be nice or encouraging.  
Assume you’re slightly skeptical and busy.  
Write your objections as a numbered list with short explanations for each.  
End with: “Here’s what would need to change for me to say yes:” and list your conditions.
