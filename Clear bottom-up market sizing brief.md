Give me a TAM, SAM, SOM breakdown for [MARKET].  
Use bottom-up math, not top-down guesses.  

1) Start by clearly defining:  
• The product/service  
• The target customer  
• The geography  
• The use case (how/when it’s used)  

2) Build TAM using bottom-up logic, such as:  
• Number of potential customers × average annual spend, or  
• Number of units used per customer × price per unit × customers  

3) Narrow to SAM by applying realistic filters:  
• Regions you can actually serve  
• Customer segments you truly fit  
• Channels you can realistically reach  

4) Define SOM as what’s realistically capturable in 3–5 years:  
• Use adoption rates, sales capacity, pricing, and conversion assumptions  

Show all formulas, intermediate steps, and final numbers.  
Explicitly list every assumption, explain why it’s reasonable, and clearly flag any that are weak, uncertain, or based on limited data.
